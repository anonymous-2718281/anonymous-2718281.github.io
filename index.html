Method

The first stage of Motion-I2V targets at deducing the motions that can plausibly animate the reference image. It is conditioned on the reference image and text prompt, and predicts the optical flow maps between the reference frame and all future frames. The second stage propagates reference image's content to synthesized frames. The novel motion-augmented temporal layer enhances 1-D temporal attention with warped features. This operation enlarges the temporal receptive field and alleviates the pressure of directly learning the complicated spatial-temporal patterns.
